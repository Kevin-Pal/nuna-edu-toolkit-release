Turn everyday ambient sound into research-ready audio datasets.

Nuna-Edu-Toolkit is a self-hostable, source-available platform for collecting and annotating first-person audio. A wearable device streams sound to your server, where it is sliced into 1-minute segments, transcribed with ASR for fast recall, and labeled through a flexible, event-driven schema — with participants choosing what to share.

The hardware

A modified wearable device

Nuna-Edu-Toolkit collects first-person audio from a commercially available smart pendant. The off-the-shelf product does not include audio recording capability — we modified it to enable first-person audio capture for research. This modification is independent of the commercial product.

How it works

From wearable to labeled dataset, in four steps.

1

Wear & capture

The wearable device captures first-person ambient audio through its microphone.

2

Stream & slice

Audio is streamed to your own server and split into 1-minute segments by day.

3

ASR pre-label

Each segment gets a speech-to-text preview so you can recall context at a glance.

4

Annotate & export

Select an event, label it (block + point), and build a first-person dataset.

Why it's useful

Built for researchers running first-person audio studies.

Swappable label schema

Event-driven, multi-granularity blocks & points. Easy to repurpose the web forms for new dataset tasks.

ASR-assisted recall

Pluggable speech-to-text — bring your own ASR model (Qwen3-ASR included) to speed up annotation and jog memory.

Privacy-first

Participants pick what to share and can delete recordings they don't want in the dataset.

Self-hostable

One Docker Compose stack, SQLite by default, all config externalized. Your data stays yours.

1-minute segments

Time-grouped browsing (hour → quarter → minute) with inline playback.
